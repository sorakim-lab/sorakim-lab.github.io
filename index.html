Hi 🙌 I map how responsibility
gets made.

I study how responsibility is structured, made legible, and stabilized in regulated sociotechnical systems. My work traces the role of procedural documents, investigative routines, and computational mediation in shaping accountability.

research snapshot focus responsibility-making in regulated organizations program AAC → CF → RC → PAC methods conceptual modeling + procedural text analysis + qualitative document analysis now building 1) CF corpus expansion + annotation reliability testing 2) responsibility convergence analysis across FDA Form 483 observations and Warning Letters
current obsession

How accountability becomes actionable before ambiguity has finished unfolding.

I am especially interested in the pressures that make responsibility appear settled too early: procedural closure, investigation routines, and the demand for administratively usable explanations.

empirical grounding

Pharmaceutical quality systems as responsibility infrastructures

SOP documentation, deviations, CAPA/RCA investigations, release processes, and inspection-facing records provide the empirical setting through which I study how accountability is organized.

CF empirical extension

Scaling the procedural text corpus

Expanding the Cognitive Friction study through a larger corpus of procedural and regulatory texts, with annotation reliability testing and feature co-occurrence analysis in progress.
