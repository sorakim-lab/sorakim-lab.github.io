Papers & Manuscripts

Papers & Manuscripts

These papers form a connected research program rather than independent works. Together they argue that responsibility is produced under constraint — across empirical HCI studies of regulated work and conceptual STS analyses of accountability, closure, and formal systems.

What's here

One research program, two layers.

The STS manuscripts explain how responsibility is organized, narrowed, and documented within formal systems. The HCI papers test those questions empirically — through prototypes and observations in pharmaceutical quality environments. Together they ask how responsibility emerges, converges, and can be redesigned.

HCI papers
Five empirical & design papers

Each HCI paper starts from a concrete friction in QC practice: retrieval burden, navigation burden, review opacity, or premature investigative closure. The empirical question is not only whether a tool is usable, but what kind of work problem it helps make visible.

STS foundations
Five theoretical foundations

The STS layer asks why those frictions matter in the first place: how responsibility is organized, narrowed, and increasingly mediated by AI within formal systems — and how these patterns extend across regulated domains.

From Retrieval to Reasoning: How SOP Accessibility Shapes Interpretation in Regulated Work
HCI paper · project-linked · Procedural Visibility in SOP Navigation

Interpretive burden is produced at the point where retrieval and interpretation become structurally decoupled in regulated work.

Shows that retrieval delays systematically shift effort from navigation to interpretation, increasing uncertainty before formal decision points.

Preprint · 2026
→ Project PDF ↗

Examines document accessibility, information retrievability, and compliance-constrained design in pharmaceutical QC environments.

  • Argument: access failure can translate into judgment difficulty — when workers cannot retrieve the right SOP, application becomes more error-prone.
  • Design contribution: usability improvements can emerge through external support layers without modifying validated source files.
  • Positions document accessibility as a substantive HCI problem in regulated environments.
paper 02

Keeping Comparison Open: Interface Scaffolding Against Premature Accountability Convergence in Regulated Investigations
HCI paper · two-study paper · Expanding Causal Reasoning + Premature Convergence under Closure Pressure

Premature causal convergence is not simply a reasoning failure, but a condition produced by investigation structure under closure pressure.

Combines two complementary studies: an empirical baseline of premature convergence (N=20) and a scaffolded intervention (N=20). 18/20 participants maintained broader comparison across alternatives; 15/20 reconsidered initial attribution after scaffolded comparison.

Preprint · 2026 · CSCW 2026 poster under review
→ RCA → PCC PDF ↗

Combines two complementary studies: a pilot observation of premature causal convergence in unassisted investigation reasoning (N=20), and a usability evaluation of a scaffold designed to counter it (N=20).

  • Pilot: initial single-cause reasoning often shifted toward broader multi-factor explanations over time.
  • Scaffold: 18/20 participants broadened causal reasoning; 15/20 reconsidered the case more broadly after scaffolded comparison.
  • Argument: investigation quality is partly a cognitive systems design problem, not only a training problem.

When Documents Don't Connect: Traceability Burden and Loopback Behavior in GMP Document Navigation
HCI paper · two-study paper · Cross-Reference Burden and Traceability Visibility + Navigation Friction and Loopback Behavior

Loopbacks and broken trace paths are structural signals of documentation burden, not merely traces of inefficient searching.

Combines cross-reference visibility with behavioral tracking to show how navigation patterns reveal hidden structural burden. 30–60% reduction in path reconstruction time; loopback frequency introduced as empirical measure of document-structural friction.

Preprint · 2026
→ Cross-ref → Navigation PDF ↗

Combines two studies: a metadata-driven cross-reference system addressing structural traceability gaps, and a behavioral navigation tracking prototype making invisible navigation friction observable.

  • Cross-reference: 30–60% reduction in path reconstruction time; higher rates of complete chain reconstruction.
  • Navigation: loopback behavior observed across all sessions — attributable to document structure rather than interface design.

HCI paper · project-linked · Review Burden and Workflow Visibility

Coordination failure is not only a communication problem, but a consequence of review burden remaining structurally unobservable.

How Regulated Systems Produce Responsibility: A Four-Layer Framework for Interaction Design
HCI concept paper · umbrella framework · connects all projects

Regulatory UX names a design space in which usability, compliance, and accountability are not separate constraints, but co-produced design conditions.

Synthesizes the HCI projects into one design argument: regulated documentation systems are active interaction environments that shape search, reasoning, coordination, and closure.

Preprint · 2026
PDF ↗

Introduces Regulatory UX as an HCI research frame for worker interaction inside compliance-constrained systems.

  • Core argument: regulated documentation systems are not neutral containers — they are active interaction environments that shape how workers search, reason, and close investigations.
  • Framework contribution: identifies four interdependent layers — accessibility, structure, coordination, and reasoning — at which system design shapes accountability conditions.
  • Connects all projects as a coherent empirical program instantiating the Regulatory UX argument across multiple failure modes.

STS conceptual manuscript

Accountability expectations are often set up before action through procedures, documentation, and evaluation rules.

Develops a structural theory of how accountability becomes aligned before execution begins — explaining why design interventions must account for prospective accountability structures, not just usability at the moment of use.

Procedural sentence structure can distribute interpretive burden and shape later judgment demands.

Operationalizes cognitive friction as a measurable sentence-level property across 500 regulatory sentences — grounding why document structure produces friction that interface redesign alone cannot solve.

Examines how procedural language structures localized judgment demands during execution and how document architecture distributes interpretive burden while preserving individual accountability.

STS conceptual manuscript

Organizational investigations often settle on individual responsibility even when failures are more distributed.

Explains the structural conditions — procedural ambiguity, closure pressure, and documentation bias — that push investigations toward identifiable actors, grounding the closure pressure scaffold the HCI work is designed to counter.

Explains why organizational investigations like root cause analysis often settle on identifiable actors even when failures are distributed across sociotechnical systems.

Premature Accountability Convergence in Regulated Organizations
STS conceptual + empirical manuscript · FDA Warning Letters N=50 · 4S Toronto 2026 under review

AI mediation can accelerate the compression of interpretive space, affecting how quickly responsibility settles.

Establishes that responsibility converges structurally in FDA Warning Letters (N=50, 160 violations, 2016–2025), with uniform firm-level attribution despite variation in framing (state: 66.2%, action: 24.4%).

Preprint · 2026 · 4S Toronto 2026 under review
PDF ↗

Examines how AI-mediated representations reshape the timing and scope of accountability. Establishes a pre-AI baseline using FDA Warning Letters (N=50, 160 violations, 2016–2025), where responsibility converges structurally regardless of causal framing.

  • Mechanisms: representational compression, epistemic authority, temporal contraction.
  • HCI relevance: motivates the RCA Reasoning Scaffolder as a counter-PAC design.
