Anticipated Accountability Convergence:
How Organizational Infrastructures Align Interpretations of Defensible Action

Working manuscript v1.1 · 2026

Develops a structural theory of how accountability expectations become aligned before action through the convergence of procedural rules, documentation regimes, and evaluative infrastructures.

  • Core question: how does accountability operate prospectively rather than only retrospectively?
  • Mechanism: procedural structuring + documentation regimes + evaluative anticipation.
  • Contribution: theorizes accountability as a pre-action organizational condition.
before action → interpretive expectations are prospectively aligned

CF during execution procedural texts

Structuring Judgment, Stabilizing Responsibility:
Cognitive Friction in Procedural Texts

Working manuscript v1.1 · 2026

Examines how procedural language structures localized judgment demands during execution and how document architecture distributes interpretive burden while preserving individual accountability.

  • Empirical pilot: 500 procedurally realistic sentences from regulatory and safety documentation.
  • Operationalization: conditional branching, cross-references, exception structures, and discretionary qualifiers.
  • Contribution: shows how responsibility-relevant judgment is embedded in procedural text architecture.
during execution → sentence structure shapes interpretive burden and attributable judgment
Empirical extension in progress: corpus expansion and annotation reliability testing are currently underway.

RC after events investigations

Responsibility Convergence in Organizational Investigations:
Structural Dynamics of Blame in Root Cause Analysis

Working manuscript v1.1 · 2026

Develops a structural theory of investigative convergence explaining how distributed causal ambiguity narrows into actor-centered accountability under closure pressure and legibility constraints.

  • Core mechanisms: procedural ambiguity, attributional closure pressure, and accountability legibility bias.
  • Focus: why system-oriented investigations still stabilize responsibility at identifiable actors.
  • Contribution: reframes blame as a structural outcome of accountability infrastructures rather than a simple cognitive bias.
after events → causal ambiguity is translated into administratively actionable responsibility
Empirical extension in progress: document analysis of FDA Warning Letters and Form 483 inspection materials.

PAC AI mediation computational acceleration

Premature Accountability Convergence:
AI-Mediated Responsibility in Sociotechnical Organizations

Working manuscript v1.1 · 2026

Investigates how AI-mediated representations reshape the timing and scope of responsibility stabilization by compressing interpretive space and accelerating convergence within accountability systems.

  • Core mechanisms: representational compression, epistemic authority amplification, and temporal contraction of sensemaking.
  • Focus: not only who becomes responsible, but how quickly and how narrowly accountability becomes actionable.
  • Contribution: shifts attention from algorithmic bias alone toward the temporal architecture of accountability.
under AI mediation → convergence may accelerate before ambiguity has been meaningfully examined
