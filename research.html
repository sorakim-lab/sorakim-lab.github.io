AAC anticipatory accountability

Anticipated Accountability Convergence (AAC)

AAC theorizes accountability as a prospective structuring condition rather than a purely retrospective evaluative event.

  • Focus: how organizations preconfigure accountable conduct before execution
  • Mechanism: procedural structuring and documentation regimes
  • Argument: interpretive expectations converge before action
before action: interpretive expectations become prospectively aligned
CF procedural texts

Cognitive Friction in Procedural Texts (CF)

CF examines how procedural language structures localized judgment demands during execution.

  • Focus: interpretive burden embedded in documentation
  • Empirical pilot: procedural text corpus
  • Method: rule-based structural annotation
during execution: procedural sentence structure shapes interpretive burden
Expanded empirical version in progress including corpus enlargement and annotation reliability testing.
RC investigations

Responsibility Convergence (RC)

RC explains why organizational investigations repeatedly stabilize accountability at identifiable actors.

  • Mechanisms: closure pressure and accountability legibility
  • Focus: investigative attribution dynamics
after events: causal ambiguity becomes administratively actionable responsibility
PAC AI mediation

Premature Accountability Convergence (PAC)

PAC analyzes how AI-mediated representations can accelerate responsibility stabilization within organizational systems.

  • Mechanisms: representational compression and epistemic authority
  • Focus: temporal acceleration of responsibility convergence
under AI mediation: convergence may occur before ambiguity is examined
Empirical extension in progress: analysis of FDA Warning Letters and Form 483 materials tracing RC-like responsibility convergence patterns in regulatory texts to extend the PAC framework beyond explicitly computational settings.
Current emphasis: responsibility, documentation, investigations, and AI-mediated accountability in regulated sociotechnical systems.
