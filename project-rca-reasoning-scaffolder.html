RCA Reasoning Scaffolder

Investigation reasoning is not a linear process — but regulated systems pressure it to become one.

// 01

Problem

Deviation investigations in GMP environments are often expected to reach closure quickly. Under that pressure, reasoning can narrow before alternative contributors have been explored. What appears as efficiency may instead be early convergence.

The result is Premature Accountability Convergence (PAC): investigation reasoning narrows toward an individual actor before procedural, contextual, and organizational contributors have been examined. This is not only a cognitive bias, but also a design problem.

// investigation pressure points
  • Closure pressure favors fast accountability over broad exploration
  • Standard forms underrepresent alternative causal pathways
  • Reasoning is documented late, after convergence has already occurred
  • Interface structure reinforces single-path explanation habits
// design implication

If investigation environments structure reasoning, then responsibility attribution is partly an interaction outcome. The question is not only who is blamed, but how the system made that attribution path easier than others.

// 02

Method

01
investigation structure analysis

Mapped recurring RCA patterns in GMP deviation workflows, focusing on early blame convergence, missing alternative contributors, and reasoning compression under closure pressure.

02
scaffold design

Designed a five-step reasoning flow that separates intake, hypothesis generation, PAC analysis, structured reasoning, and pre-closure checks to slow premature convergence.

03
rule-based pac detection + AI expansion

Built rule-based PAC detection to flag individual-blame language and surface alternative causal pathways. Added an AI expansion layer to keep reasoning space open when convergence risk was detected — not to generate answers, but to resist premature closure.

04
comparative think-aloud study · N=15

Compared participants’ initial causal interpretations with their post-scaffold reasoning, observing whether structured support changed breadth of explanation, confidence, and closure timing.

// 04

AI Integration

Most AI tools in investigation contexts are designed to accelerate closure. This layer was designed for the opposite purpose: to expand interpretive space when convergence risk appears. AI is used here as a resistance mechanism, not a decision engine.

Open AI Integration ↗
AI expansion layer · demo video

Rather than suggest a single cause, the AI layer expands underexplored branches when blame-language patterns appear.

⚠️Detects early convergence toward individual blame language
🌿Surfaces alternative contributors not yet considered
🧭Reorients reasoning toward comparison rather than answer extraction
🤝Preserves human interpretive authority over final attribution
This design treats AI as a way to counter PAC, not intensify it. The role of the model is to reopen possibility, not compress explanation.

Comparative Study

Participants first reviewed an investigation case without support, then revisited the same case using the scaffold. The goal was to observe whether structured support changed the breadth of reasoning and delayed premature closure.

The main adoption barrier was conceptual, not interactional — the interface was clear, but the idea that investigation reasoning deserves structured support was unfamiliar. The next challenge is not interface clarity, but making the value of reasoning support visible.
// 06

What This Revealed

Investigation environments do not simply record reasoning; they shape it. Under closure pressure, users can move quickly toward an accountable actor even when broader contributors remain plausible. This scaffold provides a lightweight intervention against that pressure.

This study provides evidence that a lightweight scaffold can resist that pressure. The three participants who reported no reasoning change were those with the most extensive GMP investigation experience, suggesting that the scaffold may be most useful when investigation habits are still forming.
