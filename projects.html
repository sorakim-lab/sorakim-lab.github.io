How systems shape human judgment.

Six empirical projects examining how procedural systems structure reasoning, navigation, and decision-making in GMP environments. Rather than optimizing tools, this work identifies recurring system-level conditions — closure pressure, structural opacity, and retrieval friction — and tests how minimal interventions reshape judgment and responsibility formation.

Axis 01 — Reasoning

R01 · N=15
RCA Reasoning Scaffolder
Designed to counter premature causal convergence under investigation pressure by keeping hypothesis space open during deviation analysis.
Observed expansion of causal reasoning breadth and delayed convergence across participants, indicating that closure pressure — not individual bias — structures early fixation.
R02 · N=5 (pilot)
Premature Causal Convergence
Pilot study examining how practitioners initiate RCA under time pressure.
All participants began with a single-cause hypothesis and revised it within minutes, suggesting convergence is structurally induced rather than cognitively inherent.

Axis 02 — Navigation

N01 · N=12
SOP Accessibility System
Transforms scanned SOP archives into searchable environments, reframing accessibility as a usability condition rather than infrastructure limitation.
Reduced retrieval friction and enabled faster task orientation, showing that accessibility directly shapes downstream interpretation and execution.
N02 · N=17
Cross-Reference System
Makes hidden dependencies across deviation, CAPA, and change control records visible through structured linkage.
Improved document traceability and navigation paths, redistributing cognitive load from individuals to system structure.
N03 · N=5
Cognitive Navigation Explorer
Tracks navigation behavior across procedural documents, capturing hesitation, loopbacks, and cross-reference jumps.
Revealed recurring navigation loops and friction points tied to document structure rather than user skill.
N04 · N=6
Smart Feedback Dashboard
Visualizes review cycles and feedback delays without modifying validated systems.
Made revision loops and coordination delays legible, shifting awareness from individual inefficiency to workflow structure.
