axis 01 · reasoning
How regulated systems shape causal reasoning
reasoning · 01
pilot study preprint Deployed
Keeping Comparison Open under Closure Pressure

Closure pressure compresses accountable interpretation before broader contributors can be compared — not because investigators reason poorly, but because investigation structure forecloses alternatives early.

18/20 produced broader causal accounts · 15/20 reconsidered initial attribution under scaffolded comparison · CHI 2026 submission in preparation · CSCW 2026 poster under review

A structured reasoning scaffold that keeps alternative explanations visible long enough to preserve comparison and delay premature convergence, without automating the conclusion. Intervention half of a two-study paper with the companion premature convergence study.

reasoning design · PAC counter-design · streamlit · python
reasoning · 02
pilot study timed reflection
Premature Convergence under Closure Pressure

Early closure is not always deliberate — time pressure and the need for a quickly documentable explanation push investigations toward a single explanation before alternatives are meaningfully compared.

15/20 single-cause at initial timepoint · 18/20 broadened within 10 minutes · confidence avg 1/5 regardless of experience

Observational study examining how early single-explanation accounts broaden when investigators are given more time and interpretive space. Observational half of the same two-study paper.

closure pressure · time-based observation · pilot
axis 02 · navigation
How regulated systems shape document navigation
navigation · 01
pilot study preprint Deployed
Procedural Visibility in SOP Navigation

Document inaccessibility creates interpretive burden upstream of formal error — retrieval difficulty becomes judgment difficulty before any mistake is recorded.

Access shapes interpretation: reduced retrieval time (~6 min → ~1 min) while expanding the range of procedures considered actionable

An external retrieval layer that makes scanned SOP archives searchable without altering validated source files, increasing procedural visibility at the point of need.

document UX · retrieval · streamlit · python
navigation · 02
pilot study preprint Deployed
Cross-Reference Burden and Traceability Visibility

Traceability gaps are not merely maintenance defects — they redistribute hidden connective labor onto practitioners reconstructing accountability paths under time pressure.

Loopback as a structural signal: navigation friction reflects missing or non-navigable document relationships rather than user error

A traceability layer that makes document linkage, broken paths, and lifecycle mismatches visible across document networks without modifying validated systems.

traceability · networkx · metadata mapping · streamlit · python
navigation · 03
pilot study preprint Deployed
Navigation Friction and Loopback Behavior

Loopbacks are not user error — they mark moments where document structure failed to resolve a judgment demand on the first pass.

Loopback behavior observed across all sessions — attributable to document structure rather than interface design

A behavioral tracking prototype that captures navigation paths, hesitation, and cross-reference jumps to make otherwise invisible document friction observable across sessions.

loopback tracking · behavioral data · streamlit · python
navigation · 04
pilot study preprint Deployed
Review Burden and Workflow Visibility

Coordination breakdown is produced gradually when review burden stays invisible until delay has already accumulated — the problem is structural, not behavioral.

Review delays, revision loops, and feedback burden rendered visible across sessions · participants interpreted delay as structural rather than individual · CSCW 2026 submission in preparation

A workflow visibility layer that makes review delays, revision loops, and feedback burden legible before coordination breaks down, without modifying validated systems.

workflow friction · TF-IDF · dashboard · streamlit · python
